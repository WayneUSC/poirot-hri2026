# Experimental Protocol Summary

This protocol summarizes the study flow described in the HRI 2026 paper and appendix.

## Participants and Roles

The study used a six-player murder mystery game, *Judgment Quartet*. Each participant played one fixed role:

- Fang Qi: defendant
- Tang Yali: victim's daughter
- Ye Caiyang: prosecutor
- Yin Hong: defense attorney
- Chu Mihe: nun/witness
- Chen Haotong: police officer

## Conditions

Two clue-delivery conditions were compared:

- **Physical-card condition:** POIROT delivered laminated clue cards using the physical dispenser.
- **In-app condition:** POIROT facilitated the game while clues were accessed through the companion app.

Both conditions used the same role setup, script-reading flow, and robot game-master framing.

## Flow

1. Welcome participants and explain consent/safety procedures.
2. Assign roles and confirm device setup.
3. Participants create or join a room in the iOS companion app.
4. Participants select roles.
5. Participants read their role scripts.
6. POIROT facilitates clue delivery according to condition; in the app condition, participants access clues through the iOS companion app.
7. Participants discuss, cross-check clues, and reconstruct the case.
8. POIROT releases public testimony/clue moments.
9. Participants complete post-game questionnaires.

## Questionnaires

The appendix includes:

- Narrative Immersion Scale
- Social Presence Scale
- Game Experience and Robot Interaction Items
- Negative Attitudes toward Robots Scale
- Big Five personality items
- demographic and prior-experience items

Use `paper/POIROT-HRI2026-appendix.pdf` for the exact questionnaire wording and factor-analysis summaries.
